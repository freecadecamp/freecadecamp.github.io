Our Technology

What is a Biomass Gasifier?

"A Biomass Gasifier is a machine that converts solid biomass into combustible gas."

Instead of directly burning the fuel, the gasifier converts it into a clean-burning gas called Producer Gas.

How It Works

1

Biomass fuel is loaded into the gasifier

2

A limited amount of air enters the chamber

3

Because oxygen is limited, the fuel does not burn completely — it undergoes gasification

4

Produces a mixture of gases: Carbon Monoxide, Hydrogen, Methane — forming Producer Gas that burns with a clean blue flame

Producer Gas Composition

Carbon Monoxide (CO) Hydrogen (H₂) Methane (CH₄)

Key Benefits

  • Reduces LPG and diesel consumption
  • Produces clean smokeless flame
